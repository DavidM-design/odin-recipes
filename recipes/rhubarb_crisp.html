Rhubarb Crisp

Description

Commonly paired with strawberries the rhubarb pie is a quintessential spring dessert. When the spring's first rhubarb shows up in its young and fresh state, you can trim it in seconds. If it has fibrous outer strings, peel them off as you would those of celery. Toss the rhubarb in with some orange or lemon juice and zest, and a litle sugar. (This is where you can also sub in strawberries for some pieces of the rhubarb) Blend the ingredients for the crisp topping in a food processor, crumble the topping over the rhubarb mixture, and bake -- it is nerly effortless and as good or better than a pie

Ingredients

Yield: 6 to 8 servings

  • 6 tablespoons cold butter, cut into small pieces, plus more for greasing pan
  • 2 1/2 to 3 poinds rhubarb, trimmed, tough strings removed, and cut into 1 1/2-inch pieces (about 5 to 6 cups)
  • 1/4 cup white sugar
  • 1 tablespoon orange or lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon orange or lemon zest
  • 3/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon connamon, or to taste
  • pinch salt
  • 1/2 cup rolled oats
  • 1/2 cup pecans

Steps

  1. Heat oven to 375°F. Grease an 8- or 9-inch square baking or gratin dish with a little butter. Toss rhubarb with white sugar, orange or lemon juice and zest, and spread in baking dish.
  2. Put the 6 tablespoons butter in a food processsor along with brown sugar, flour, cinnamon and salt, and pulse for about 20 or 30 seconds, until it looks like small peas and just begins to clump together. Add oats and pecans and pulse just a few times to combine
  3. Crumble the topping over rhubarb and bake until golden and beginning to brown, 45 to 50 minutes
